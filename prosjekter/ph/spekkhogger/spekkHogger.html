Meet the Ocean’s Top Predator: Orcas!

Did you know orcas, also called killer whales, are actually the largest members of the dolphin family? These black-and-white beauties are incredibly smart and powerful creatures that roam the world’s oceans, often in family groups called “pods.” Each pod is like a close-knit family, and they work together to hunt, play, and explore.


Amazing Hunters

Orcas are incredible hunters! They eat a variety of sea creatures like fish, squid, seals, and even other whales. Orcas use teamwork and special techniques to catch their food. Sometimes, they work together to create waves that can knock seals off ice floes! Each pod has its own special tricks for hunting, and young orcas learn by watching the adults in their group.


Underwater Talkers

Orcas “talk” to each other using clicks, whistles, and calls. They have unique sounds for each pod, almost like a special language that only their family can understand. This helps them stick together and communicate while swimming through the ocean. Orcas can even identify each other’s voices, just like we recognize our friends' voices on the phone!


Super Swimmers

Orcas are built for speed and agility in the water! They can swim as fast as 35 mph, making them one of the fastest animals in the ocean. Their powerful tails help propel them through the waves, while their sleek, torpedo-shaped bodies cut through the water with ease. But orcas don’t just swim—they love to jump, flip, and splash around, sometimes leaping high out of the water in what’s called “breaching.” Scientists think they do this for different reasons: to communicate with other orcas, to shake off parasites, or just to have fun!


Orcas also glide gracefully just below the surface, making them great at sneaking up on their prey. Sometimes, they travel hundreds of miles in search of food or new waters. With their amazing swimming skills, orcas can dive as deep as 1,000 feet to explore the ocean depths, but they still need to come up for air every 3-5 minutes.
